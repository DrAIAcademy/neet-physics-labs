Formula Explanation

Ohm's Law is valid for an ohmic conductor when physical conditions like temperature remain constant.

Ohm's Law: V = IR Current: I = V/R Resistance: R = V/I Power: P = VI = I²R = V²/R Series: Rs = R1 + R2 + R3 + ... Parallel: 1/Rp = 1/R1 + 1/R2 + 1/R3 + ... Series: same current through all resistors. Parallel: same voltage across each branch.

NEET key: Ammeter is connected in series and voltmeter is connected in parallel.

Simple NEET Explanation

  • Ohm's Law says voltage is directly proportional to current if temperature remains constant.
  • Resistance opposes current flow.
  • If voltage increases and resistance is constant, current increases.
  • If resistance increases and voltage is constant, current decreases.
  • Ammeter is connected in series because it measures current through the circuit.
  • Voltmeter is connected in parallel because it measures potential difference across a component.
  • Conventional current flows from positive terminal to negative terminal.
  • Electrons flow from negative terminal to positive terminal.
  • In series circuits, same current flows through all resistors.
  • In parallel circuits, same voltage appears across all branches.

NEET Traps

  • Ammeter is always connected in series.
  • Voltmeter is always connected in parallel.
  • Conventional current and electron flow are opposite.
  • Ohm's Law is valid only when physical conditions like temperature remain constant.
  • V-I graph of an ohmic conductor is a straight line.
  • Slope of V-I graph gives resistance if V is on Y-axis and I is on X-axis.
  • In series combination, current is same but voltage divides.
  • In parallel combination, voltage is same but current divides.
  • Equivalent resistance in series is greater than individual resistances.
  • Equivalent resistance in parallel is less than the smallest individual resistance.
  • Higher resistance does not mean higher current; it means lower current for same voltage.

NEET-Style Observation Questions

  2. What happens to current when resistance is doubled and voltage is constant?

  4. Why is voltmeter connected in parallel?

  6. In series circuit, which quantity remains same: current or voltage?
  7. In parallel circuit, which quantity remains same: current or voltage?
